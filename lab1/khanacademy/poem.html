Between Who I Was and Who I’m Becoming

I walk a road with yesterday’s dust
Still clinging softly to my shoes,
Dreams behind me, sharp with rust,
New ones ahead I haven’t used.

I carry doubts like quiet stones,
Hidden deep inside my chest,
Yet hope still hums within my bones,
A restless fire that won’t let rest.

The world speaks loud, I answer low,
Learning strength in gentle tone,
I’m not the man I used to know,
Nor yet the king I’ll call my own.

So here I stand, mid-breath, mid-flight,
A heart in motion, not in chains,
Growing slowly toward the light,
Through unanswered prayers and pains.
